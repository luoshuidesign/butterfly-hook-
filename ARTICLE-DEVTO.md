## I Tried It

**Apple's waters.**

Not just `background: #ffffff`. The *why*: Apple's designers believe product images should be the only protagonist. Any decorative element is noise. That's not a color scheme. That's faith.

**Stripe's waters.**

Not just `color: #533afd`. Stripe's 14,386-pixel page calculates every CTA button's cubic-bezier curve with precision. That's not frontend code. That's conversion rate engineering. That purple isn't "pretty." It survived thousands of A/B tests.

**Linear's waters.**

Not just `background: #08090a`. Why not pure black #000000? Because OLED screens create ghosting when pixels fully turn off. That's not a color choice. That's physics. 64px headlines with -1.408px letter-spacing — tuned by a designer's eyes, one pixel at a time.
